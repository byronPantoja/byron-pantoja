# I Built 3 Production-Quality NextJS Sites in 10 Days Using AI — Here's the Plan

Most developer portfolios are a single page with a few links. I needed something different — I needed to prove to employers that I could ship real, functional web applications, not just talk about them. So I set a goal: build three distinct NextJS projects, deploy them on Vercel, and tie them together with a portfolio site, all in 10 days.

The secret weapon? AI-assisted development with Claude.

## Why I Did This

I'm a web developer based in Davao, Philippines with 14+ years of experience at Coffee For Peace, a family social enterprise. Most of that time was spent in operations, brand strategy, and WordPress. I needed to show employers I could build modern NextJS/React applications — and I needed to show them fast.

The portfolio sprint wasn't about learning NextJS from scratch. It was about proving I could **ship** — quickly, cleanly, and at a professional level. The kind of speed that matters to an employer looking for a reliable remote developer.

## The Strategy: Three Projects, Three Skill Sets

I chose three projects that each target a different type of work employers actually hire for:

1. **A service business landing page** — marketing sites with interactive UI (booking calendars, contact forms)
2. **A headless Shopify storefront** — e-commerce with real API integration
3. **A full-stack operations dashboard** — internal tools with authentication, databases, and data tables

Together, they tell an employer: "This person can build my landing page, my store, AND my internal tools."

## The 10-Day Schedule

I blocked out 6 focused hours each morning (6 AM – 12 PM PHT) and assigned each project a 3-day window with 1 day for the portfolio site.

- **Days 1–3:** Serenity Studio (salon landing page)
- **Days 4–6:** That's G (Shopify storefront)
- **Days 7–9:** BaseLine (operations dashboard)
- **Day 10:** byronpantoja.com (portfolio site tying it all together)

In practice, the salon site shipped in roughly one session. The Shopify store took two days. The dashboard — which grew far beyond the original scope into a full-stack Supabase application — took about two days as well.

## How I Used AI as a Development Partner

This is the part most people get wrong about AI-assisted development. I didn't ask Claude to "build me a website." I used it as a planning and scaffolding partner in a deliberate loop:

1. **Plan with Claude** — define the sitemap, component list, design direction, and copy
2. **Build in Claude Code** — scaffold components, generate boilerplate, debug errors
3. **Return to Claude for audits** — screenshot the deployed site, get a checklist of fixes
4. **Fix and ship** — apply the fixes, push, redeploy

The AI handled boilerplate, mock data, copy generation, and debugging. I made the architecture decisions, design choices, and quality calls. The result was senior-level output at an accelerated pace.

## What I Learned

**Scope discipline matters.** At one point I considered adding Mindbody API integration to the salon site. Claude redirected me — the booking calendar UI already demonstrated the skill without the integration overhead. Keeping mockups demo-quality (not production-complete) was the right call for a portfolio sprint.

**The priority order saved me.** When things got tight, I knew which project mattered most: the Shopify store (live API data is the most impressive signal), then the dashboard, then the salon. A portfolio with two strong mockups beats three rushed ones.

**AI-assisted speed is a feature, not a footnote.** When I write to employers, I lead with what's possible now with AI-assisted development. It's not about replacing skill — it's about multiplying output.

## The Result

Three deployed sites, three GitHub repos with detailed READMEs, and a dark editorial portfolio at [byronpantoja.com](https://byronpantoja.com) connecting them all. Each README explains the build process, the AI workflow, and the technical decisions — because employers read READMEs.

If you're a developer looking to build a portfolio fast, the approach works: narrow your platform (I chose OnlineJobs.ph only), narrow your stack (NextJS only), and use AI to compress the timeline without compressing the quality.
